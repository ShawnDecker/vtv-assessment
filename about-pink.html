The P.I.N.K. Framework

Your value is not determined by your income, your appearance, or your follower count. It is measured by how you steward what you have been given. P.I.N.K. is a faith-based framework built on the principle that every person manages five areas of life — and how you handle them defines your trajectory.

P
People
How you invest in relationships
I
Influence
How you lead and impact others
N

K

T
Time
How you steward your hours
"His master said to him, 'Well done, good and faithful servant. You have been faithful over a little; I will set you over much.'" Matthew 25:23
The Foundation

The Parable of the Talents teaches that God entrusts each person with resources — not equally, but intentionally. What matters is not how much you start with, but what you do with it.

P.I.N.K. measures your stewardship across five domains. Not to judge you, but to show you where you are strong, where you are weak, and where your next breakthrough is waiting.

This is not a personality test. This is not a compatibility quiz. This is a mirror.

How the Assessment Works
1
25 questions, 5 minutes. Each question maps to one of the five pillars and a specific sub-category within it.
2
Your score is calculated. Each pillar gets a score from 0-100. Your master score is the weighted average across all five.
3
You get a diagnostic report. Strengths, weaknesses, a heatmap of every sub-category, and a personalized prescription for growth.
4
You take action. Action plans, challenges, coaching, and tools — all built around your specific weak points.
People

How you build, maintain, and invest in the relationships around you. Family, friends, community, team. Are you pouring in or pulling away?

Sub-categories: Communication, Boundaries, Conflict Resolution, Support, Trust

Influence

How you lead, serve, and shape the world around you. Not followers — impact. Are you making the room better when you walk in?

Sub-categories: Leadership, Service, Vision, Accountability, Mentoring

Numbers

How you handle money, debt, savings, and financial decisions. Not how much you have — how wisely you manage it.

Sub-categories: Budgeting, Debt Management, Generosity, Income Growth, Financial Planning

Knowledge

How you learn, grow, and apply wisdom. Are you still a student of life, or have you stopped reading the manual?

Sub-categories: Self-Education, Skill Building, Adaptability, Critical Thinking, Spiritual Growth

Time

How you spend your hours, days, and years. Everyone gets 24 hours. The question is whether yours are intentional or accidental.

Sub-categories: Prioritization, Rest, Productivity, Presence, Long-Term Planning
