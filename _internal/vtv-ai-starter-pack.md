## 1 · Time Audit (Time pillar — the multiplier)

> Act as a time-leverage coach. I'm going to describe my last 7 days in 10 lines.
> For each line: tell me what pillar it served (People / Influence / Numbers / Knowledge / Time-itself),
> and rate it 0.1x to 2.0x on the Time Multiplier scale (where 1.0x = neutral, <1.0x = drained value, >1.0x = compounded value).
> Then identify my single biggest leak and the single highest-leverage swap I could make this week.
>
> [paste your 10 lines below]

---

## 2 · People Map (People pillar)

> Act as a relationship-equity strategist. List the 10 people who got the most of my time/attention/money this past month.
> For each: rate (a) energy contribution to me 0–10, (b) energy I contributed to them 0–10,
> (c) directional ROI for the relationship.
> Then surface the one underinvested relationship that should get 2x my attention next month, and the one over-invested
> relationship I should rebalance.
>
> [paste your list below]

---

## 3 · Influence Inventory (Influence pillar)

> Act as a positioning consultant. Below is what I've published, said publicly, or contributed to in the last 90 days.
> For each item: identify (a) which pillar it strengthened my authority in, (b) the specific outcome it produced
> (a meeting / a follower / a customer / nothing measurable / unknown).
> Then surface the one piece of work that punched above its weight — and the format I should repeat next month.
>
> [paste your list below]

---

## 4 · Numbers Diagnostic (Numbers pillar)

> Act as a CFO. Here are 5 numbers from my business or personal finances over the last 30 days:
> revenue, expenses, hours worked, leads in, customers out.
> Compute (a) gross margin, (b) effective hourly rate, (c) close rate, (d) per-hour profit.
> Then tell me the single number that, if I improved it 10%, would compound the most across the others.
>
> [paste your numbers below]

---

## 5 · Knowledge Compounding (Knowledge pillar)

> Act as a personal-development librarian. Below is a list of what I've read, watched, listened to, or studied in the last 30 days.
> For each: identify (a) which pillar it served, (b) whether I actually applied it (yes/no/partial),
> (c) whether the source was original or a derivative summary.
> Then identify the one piece of knowledge I haven't fully implemented yet that would 2x return on the time I've already spent learning it.
>
> [paste your list below]

---

## 6 · Decision Matrix (P.I.N.K.T. cross-pillar)

> Act as a decision coach using the VTV P.I.N.K.T. framework.
> I'm trying to decide: [describe the decision in one sentence].
> For each option, score the impact on each pillar (-3 to +3):
> - People: how does this affect my key relationships?
> - Influence: how does this affect my authority/positioning?
> - Numbers: what's the financial / time / energy cost?
> - Knowledge: does this build skill that compounds?
> - Time: does this free time, neutral, or consume it?
> Total each option, then surface the option that maximizes Time Multiplier × the others.
>
> [describe the decision below]

---

## 7 · Weekly P.I.N.K.T. Review (every Sunday)

> Run my weekly P.I.N.K.T. review.
> For each pillar (People / Influence / Numbers / Knowledge / Time):
> Score 0–10 on this week's progress, then identify the next-week 1-thing-only commitment.
> End with a Time Multiplier estimate for the upcoming week and the single accountability prompt I should set for myself.
>
> [paste this week's brief notes per pillar below]

## How to use these

1. Pick the pillar you want to move first. Start with **Time** if unsure.
2. Paste the prompt into your AI assistant of choice.
3. Spend 5 minutes filling in your real data.
4. Apply the single recommendation it surfaces. Skip the rest for now.
5. Re-run weekly. Track which pillar moves your Master Value Score the most.
