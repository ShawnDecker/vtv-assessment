## Reflection

In the Introduction of *Running From Miracles*, Shawn writes: "Of all the things I have learned in my life, the most important is that God disguises miracles. In fact, if you're not looking, you may just call it a coincidence."

This book begins with a simple but powerful truth — God is working in the details of your life right now. Not in the spectacular, but in the supernatural disguised as the ordinary. What looks like a random Tuesday might be the setup for the greatest breakthrough of your life. But you'll miss it if you're looking for fireworks instead of the still small voice.

Shawn's transparency about his struggles — sexual addiction, depression, divorce, financial ruin, thoughts of suicide — isn't meant to shock. It's meant to say: "If God can work miracles in THIS mess, He can work them in yours too."

## Prayer

God, open my eyes today. I've been looking for the spectacular when You've been working in the supernatural all along. Help me see the miracles I've been walking past — the conversations, the doors, the quiet moments where You were right there. I stop running. I turn around. Show me what You've been doing behind the scenes. In Jesus' name, Amen.

## Action Step

Set a 5-minute timer and sit in complete silence. No phone, no music, no agenda. Just listen. When the timer goes off, write down whatever came to mind — even if it seems random. God speaks in the quiet.
