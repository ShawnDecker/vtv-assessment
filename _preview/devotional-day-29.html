Since my son was born about three days after I shipped out, they allowed me to go home about two weeks early. The trip back was so long. We had docked in the lower southern section of Spain. They then put us in a helicopter and flew us about one hour inland with a bunch of our gear we would need to set up when we got back. Then we got on a C130 (Chop Job) and flew to Portugal. We went from one hundred and sixteen degree temperatures on the flight deck to sixty degrees in Aires, Portugal. We stayed the night there and got up the next morning for our flight home, which was fourteen hours, flying from Aires, Portugal to Brunswick, Maine. Reflection

In Chapter 29, "Traveling the World," Shawn writes: "He used one of the smaller needles to put an injection in, but about a third of the way through, it broke." This chapter of Running From Miracles reveals how God was working even in the middle of what looked like chaos. As you read today, look for the places in your own life where God has been quietly showing up — disguising miracles as ordinary moments.
