A Closer Look
Why MBTI Falls Short ▼
The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator is the most popular personality test in the world — and one of the least reliable. Studies show 39–76% of test-takers get a different result when they retake it.
  • It gives you a type label (INFJ, ENTP) but no action plan
  • It doesn't measure weaknesses — just preferences
  • The scientific community has widely labeled it pseudoscience
  • It flatters instead of diagnosing — everyone's result feels good
  • No financial, time, or relationship dimensions
Why Enneagram Isn't Enough ▼
The Enneagram is a rich personality framework with deep psychological roots — but it's too complex for most people to act on and missing critical dimensions.
  • 9 types, 27 subtypes, wings, growth/stress arrows — hard to apply
  • Highly subjective — your type depends on who's interpreting
  • No action plan — you learn your pattern but not what to do about it
  • No financial, time management, or career dimensions
  • Focuses on motivation, not outcomes
Why CliftonStrengths Misses the Point ▼
Gallup's CliftonStrengths (formerly StrengthsFinder) is a best-seller in corporate L&D. But it has a fatal flaw: it only measures strengths.
  • Deliberately ignores weaknesses — tells you to "lean into" what you're good at
  • Costs $60+ for the basic report, $100+ for the full 34
  • No personal life dimensions — only professional strengths
  • Snapshot assessment — not designed for retesting or tracking improvement
  • No follow-up system, coaching reports, or action plans
