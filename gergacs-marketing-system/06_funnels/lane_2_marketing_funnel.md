## What gets measured

### Per piece (track in Notion Lighthouse)
- Reach / impressions
- Engagement (saves, shares, comments)
- Click-throughs to bio link or specific URL
- DMs received with keyword (PEARL/MOVE/PICK/etc.)
- New followers attributable to this piece
- Newsletter subs attributable

### Per channel weekly
- Net follower change
- Top-3 posts by engagement
- Top-3 posts by conversion (DMs, clicks)
- Cost per acquired follower (if any boost spend)

### Compounding metric: audience pool
Total addressable audience = IG followers + YT subs + Newsletter subs + LinkedIn connections + TikTok followers + Facebook followers
Track as a single number weekly; this is the inventory Lane 1 retargets.

## Content-to-conversion lag

Lane 2 doesn't generate same-week revenue. Realistic lag:
- **Week 1-2:** No measurable conversion. Audience-building only.
- **Week 3-4:** First DMs from content; first newsletter-driven booked consults
- **Month 2-3:** Retargeting starts working as a 2nd touch
- **Month 4-12:** Compounding effect — content from month 1 is still pulling DMs and conversions

This is why Lane 2 cannot be optimized purely on this-month KPIs. It builds the audience that Lanes 1 and 3 monetize over the next 12 months.

## Anti-pattern flags

- ❌ Posting without UTM tagging — you can't measure what you can't tag
- ❌ Posting cross-platform identical content (each platform has its own register; Repurpose.io is for Reels-to-Shorts cross-posts, not for "post to everything from one button")
- ❌ Optimizing for vanity metrics (likes) without clicks/DMs
- ❌ Pearl metaphor in 80%+ of content (saturation kills the metaphor)
- ❌ Generic stock photography (must use Savannah Image Library directives)
