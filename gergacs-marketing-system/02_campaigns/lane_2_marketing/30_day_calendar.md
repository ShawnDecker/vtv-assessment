## Week 1 — Hero topic: "What an appraiser sees that an agent misses"

**Why this hero:** Highest-leverage credential play. Differentiator-led. Sets the tone for the campaign.

### YouTube long-form (Thursday drop)
**Title:** "What I See in 60 Seconds Walking a Savannah Home — From a Realtor + Appraiser"
**Length:** 8-10 min
**Outline:**
1. Quick intro — Nikki, dual credential
2. Walkthrough of one Savannah listing (recent, with permission), narrating in real-time
3. Three things she'd flag for a buyer (foundation, water, finish quality)
4. Three things she'd flag for a seller (condition vs price)
5. Why this matters in Savannah specifically — historic, coastal, weather considerations
6. CTA: book a Pre-List Valuation OR Buyer Discovery on Microsoft Bookings

1. "POV: I'm an appraiser walking the home you're about to bid on"

3. "What 21 years of appraising taught me about Savannah waterfront pricing"

## Anti-pattern flags for Lane 2

- ❌ Posting just to post (no clear persona, hook, or CTA stage)
- ❌ Re-using competitor imagery or generic stock real-estate photography (use `01_strategy/savannah_image_library.md`)
- ❌ Letting blog posts go up untagged for taxonomy and meta description
- ❌ Pearl metaphor in 80%+ of content — saturation kills the metaphor
- ❌ Multi-CTA confusion ("buy or sell or get an appraisal!")

## Lane 2 → Lane 1 handoff

Every Lane 2 piece that performs above 2x average reach gets:
1. A retargeting audience created (people who engaged) → fed into Lane 1 ad sets
2. A "second wind" promotion — boost spend $50-100 on the post itself
3. Surfaced in next week's Monday Brief as "do more of this"

The interlock is what makes the system compound.
