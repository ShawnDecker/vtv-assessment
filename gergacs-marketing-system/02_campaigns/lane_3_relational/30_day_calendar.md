# Lane 3 · Relational · 30-Day Calendar

**Goal:** Reactivate the past-client SOI, collect reviews systematically, and host one community event.

**Targets:**
- 5 personal touches per week from Nikki to past clients (20 over 30 days)
- 10+ Google + Zillow + Facebook reviews collected
- 2-4 referrals received and tagged in BoldTrail
- 1 client-appreciation event held (8-12 attendees)
- 3-5 past clients in active reactivation conversations

**Owners:** Nikki personally (this lane is *only* her). Muhammed handles logistics + Trello reminders.

---

## Pre-flight (Week 0 — 1 hour total)

- [ ] Pull SOI list from BoldTrail (export contacts tagged `client-past`, `referral`, or in `Closed` stage)
- [ ] Sort by closing date (oldest first — those whose 7-year mark is approaching)
- [ ] Identify 20-30 priority touch targets for the month
- [ ] Block 30 minutes Wednesday + Friday on Nikki's calendar for the personal-touch ritual
- [ ] Block one weekend evening at month-end for the client event

## Week 1 · Reactivation Wave + Review Sweep

### Personal touches (5 emails, Wednesday)

Nikki sends 5 personal emails to past clients. Each is custom — no template. Format:

```
Subject: [something specific to them — not "Just checking in"]

Body: 60-90 words.
- One specific reference (their kid's recent win, a neighborhood update, a market insight relevant to THEIR home)
- One sentence about what's new on Nikki's end (only if relevant)
- No ask. No CTA. The relationship is the deliverable.
```

**Use `gergacs-followup-composer` agent** with stage = `Past Client (any)` and the specific signal you're referencing.

### Review sweep (Friday)

For every closed deal in the past 90 days that hasn't left a review:

```
Subject: A small ask, if you have a minute
```

```
[Client name],

Hard to believe it's been [X months] since you got the keys to [address / neighborhood reference].

If you have a minute, the single best thing past clients can do for me is leave a quick review on the platform that's easiest for you. I'm linked on each one — just one or two sentences is plenty:

📍 Google: [Google Business Profile review link]
🏠 Zillow: [Zillow agent profile review link]
📘 Facebook: [Gergacs Realty FB review link]

If something's been on your mind that wasn't perfect — please tell me directly first. I'd rather hear it from you than read about it elsewhere, and I'd want to make it right.

Either way: thank you for trusting me with one of the biggest decisions of your life.

— Nikki
```

**Send to:** Every Closed-stage client past 90 days with no review currently surfaced.
**Track:** BoldTrail tag `review-requested-2026-04` for the cohort.

### Trello tasks (Muhammed)

- [ ] Build the "Wednesday Personal Touch" recurring Trello card (5 names per week, rotated)
- [ ] Pull the past-90-days closed-deal list for Friday review sweep
- [ ] Verify Google, Zillow, and Facebook review URLs are live (if any are missing, this is a bigger problem — flag for emergency fix)

## Week 2 · Referral Activation Wave

### Personal touches (5 emails, Wednesday — different 5 names)

Same format as Week 1. Different names. Track in BoldTrail.

### Referral conversation activation (Friday)

For any past client who has *previously mentioned* a friend or family member "thinking of moving" or "thinking of selling" — call them personally within 48 hours.

Script:
> "Hey [client name], it's Nikki — a quick call. You mentioned [person] back in [month/year] was thinking about [moving / selling / downsizing]. I wanted to circle back and check in. If they're still considering it, I'd love an introduction whenever the timing's right. No pressure on you — just don't want to lose touch with someone you care about."

**Outcome:** A warm intro to a prospect who's already been pre-vetted by someone Nikki has a real relationship with. These convert at 3-5x the rate of cold leads.

### Trello tasks

- [ ] Pull all BoldTrail notes containing keywords "thinking about" "considering" "friend" "family" "their parents" — surface 5-10 referral opportunities

## Week 3 · Event Planning + Continued Touches

### Personal touches (5 emails — different 5 names)

Same format. Now 15 names touched cumulatively.

### Coastal Coffee event invite goes out

**Concept:** "Coastal Coffee with Nikki" — a Friday morning, 8:30-10 AM event at a Savannah coffee shop or her office porch. 8-12 past clients invited. No agenda. Just coffee, conversation, oysters or pastries, time together.

**Why:** Past clients in a room together create their own conversation about Savannah life — and your name comes up naturally between them. The event also generates content (with permission): photos, short Reels, maybe a "Coastal Coffee" video series for YouTube.

**Invite (Outlook send to 12-20 past clients):**

```
Subject: Coffee & oysters · last Friday in May

[Client name],

I'm hosting a small Friday-morning gathering at [location] — coffee, oysters, maybe a chocolate pastry that won't change your life but won't disappoint you either. No agenda. Just past clients I've stayed close with and the people they brought into the conversation.

Friday, May [date], 8:30-10 AM.

If you can swing by for 30 minutes (or 90), it would mean a lot. RSVP whenever — Microsoft Bookings link below to grab a spot, or just reply with a yes.

[Bookings link]

— Nikki
```

## Lane 3 follow-up cadence (post-campaign integration)

After the 30-day campaign ends, Lane 3 becomes ongoing:
- **Week-of cadence:** 5 personal touches, every Wednesday
- **Quarterly:** Coastal Coffee or comparable event, 8-12 attendees
- **Annually:** Closing-anniversary card to every past client (Make Scenario 05 from ops package handles this)

This is the highest-margin lane in the business. Closed deals from referrals carry roughly 6-9x the margin of paid-lead deals because the acquisition cost is essentially zero.

---

## What kills Lane 3

- ❌ Any sign of a templated mass-email feel (immediate trust collapse)
- ❌ Asks layered onto every touch (the touch itself is the deliverable; the ask comes only when there's a real reason)
- ❌ Forgetting a specific detail you previously knew about a client (refer to BoldTrail notes before sending — this is non-negotiable)
- ❌ Overdoing event volume (one event/quarter is right; weekly events are exhausting and dilute the experience)
- ❌ Reviewing for review's sake — the review request must come AFTER a moment of obvious value to the client, not on a fixed schedule with no context
