Every template is USPAP-compliant. Each tells you the persona, channel, structure, and a sample populated draft.

## Template 1 · Three Quiet [Things] Reel (IG)

**Persona:** Marcus or Eleanor
**Stage:** Top of funnel
**Channel:** IG Reel + TikTok cross-post

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: Hook ("Three quiet [things] in [neighborhood] no one tells transplants about")
- 3-15s: Walk-by or B-roll of place 1, voiceover
- 15-30s: Place 2
- 30-45s: Place 3
- 45-50s: Pearl-mark ending + soft CTA

**Sample populated:**
> "Three quiet places to read on a Sunday in Ardsley Park. The bench under the live oak on 51st. The corner table at the back of [coffee shop name]. The marsh-facing porch at [restaurant]. Pearl awaits — DM me LOCAL for the rest of my list."

## Template 3 · Honest Comparison Carousel (IG + FB)

**Persona:** Eleanor (relocator) or Bill & Sandra (right-sizer)
**Stage:** Mid-funnel
**Channel:** IG carousel (8 slides), FB native upload

**Structure (8 slides):**
- Slide 1: Hook + the comparison ("Pooler vs Richmond Hill")
- Slide 2: Slide setup ("3 honest tradeoffs")
- Slide 3: Tradeoff #1 — Taxes
- Slide 4: Tradeoff #2 — Drive time + airport access
- Slide 5: Tradeoff #3 — Lifestyle
- Slide 6: Who Pooler is right for
- Slide 7: Who Richmond Hill is right for
- Slide 8: CTA + sign-off

**Sample slide 3 (Taxes):**
> *Pooler:* Chatham County · ~ 11.5 mills inside city · Special Service District
> *Richmond Hill:* Bryan County · ~ 8.8 mills · Lower base, higher growth (verify current)
> *The honest answer:* Bryan saves you $2-4k/year on a $650k home. But airport drive is 25 min vs 12.

## Template 6 · Pre-List Comparable Analysis Email (Outlook)

**Persona:** Carolyn
**Stage:** Bottom of funnel
**Channel:** Outlook · sent post-Microsoft-Bookings consult

**Structure:**
- Subject: "Your pre-list comparable bracket — [neighborhood]"
- Opening: 1-line acknowledgment of the conversation
- Middle: Reference 3-5 comparable sales (anonymized addresses or MLS#) and what they show
- Close: invitation to discuss listing strategy on the next call
- USPAP disclaimer in footer

**Sample populated body:**
> [Client name],
>
> Thank you for the conversation this morning. As discussed, here's the comparable bracket your home falls into based on recent Ardsley Park sales:
>
> - Comp 1: [MLS#] — 2,150 sqft, original heart-pine, sold last month at $XXX
> - Comp 2: [MLS#] — 2,300 sqft, updated kitchen, sold 3 months ago at $XXX
> - Comp 3: [MLS#] — 2,050 sqft, similar condition, sold 6 weeks ago at $XXX
>
> The bracket the comparables support is roughly $XXX–$XXX. Where exactly your home positions inside that bracket depends on the listing prep we discussed (the bath update timing, the back-porch refinish).
>
> Let me know if you'd like to walk through listing strategy on Friday — pick a slot here: [Microsoft Bookings].
>
> — Nikki
>
> *Pre-list comparable analysis is a strategic pricing review, not a USPAP appraisal. For estate, divorce, or lending purposes, an appraisal report is required.*

## Template 11 · Service Differentiator LinkedIn Post

**Persona:** Eleanor or Carolyn (LinkedIn-active)
**Channel:** LinkedIn long-form

**Structure:**
- Hook: a counterintuitive observation
- Body: 100-200 words backing it up with experience
- Close: invitation to think differently about an existing assumption
- (No CTA on LinkedIn — let the reader come to you)

**Sample:**
> 21 years of appraising the Lowcountry has taught me one thing every relocator needs to hear:
>
> The neighborhood you fall in love with on Zillow is rarely the one you'd buy in if you'd seen it for a Saturday morning AND a Tuesday afternoon AND a Sunday evening.
>
> Light changes. Traffic changes. Whether the dog walkers seem to know each other changes. Whether someone is grilling in October changes.
>
> When I work with relocators, the first 30 minutes of our discovery call is always: tell me what kind of Tuesday afternoon you want. The rest follows.
>
> If you're moving to Savannah and trying to make this decision from your laptop, find someone whose first question is about your Tuesday — not your timeline.

## Template 12 · "What's Your Home Worth" Reframe (Blog or YouTube)

**Persona:** Carolyn
**Stage:** Top-mid funnel (educational, builds trust, reframes the pricing conversation)

**Why this template matters:** It's the highest-converting topic for sellers. But the framing must be USPAP-safe.

**Structure (blog 800-1200 words OR YouTube 8-12 min):**
1. Hook (the wrong question vs the right question)
2. The problem with "what's your home worth?" — three reasons it's a misleading question
3. The right starting question: "what does the recent comparable bracket support?"
4. How an appraiser-trained eye reads comparables differently
5. What sellers should expect from a pricing conversation
6. Disclaimer + CTA

**USPAP-safe headline options:**
- ✅ "What 'What's Your Home Worth' Actually Means"
- ✅ "Why I Don't Tell Sellers What Their Home Is Worth"
- ✅ "The Comparable Bracket Conversation: How An Appraiser Thinks About Pricing"
- ❌ "Get Your Home's True Value" (banned)
- ❌ "Find Out What Your Home Is Really Worth" (banned)

**Closing line:**
> "If you're thinking about selling and want to walk through the comparable bracket your home falls into, book a 30-minute pre-list comparable analysis below. *(Not a USPAP appraisal — a strategic pricing review.)*"

## How to use the template library

1. Pick the template that matches your **persona × stage × channel**
2. Populate with specifics (neighborhood, address, persona reference)
3. Run through `gergacs-voice-keeper` if you want a final polish
4. Run through `gergacs-post-writer` if you need help filling in
5. Verify USPAP safety: any pricing language → frame as comparables, methodology, analysis. Never outcome.

If a piece doesn't match a template, that's a flag the format is unproven. Test it as a one-off; if it pulls, build a 13th template.
