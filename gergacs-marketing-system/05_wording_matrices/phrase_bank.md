## Pearl metaphor — the right way to use it

The pearl carries the brand. Use it where it lands. Avoid forcing it.

### Natural uses

- "Pearl found:" (listing announcement)
- "Your next pearl awaits" (sign-off, IG bio)
- "A pearl on the marsh" (waterfront listing)
- "Pearl Series" (recurring content)
- "Find your pearl" (mid-funnel ask)
- "The pearl is patient. So is the right home."
- "Coastal Coffee" (event name — pearl-adjacent)

### Forced uses (avoid)

- "Investing in your future pearl" ❌
- "Pearl-quality service" ❌
- "Pearls of wisdom" ❌
- "Pearl by pearl" (too cute) ❌

When in doubt: use the metaphor 1x per piece, not 3x.

## Voice modifiers (use generously)

These small words and phrases stack the brand voice without sounding scripted:

- "Honestly..."
- "Quietly..."
- "Patient" / "patiently"
- "Refined"
- "Hospitable"
- "Boutique"
- "Genuine" / "genuinely"
- "Calm"
- "Specific" (vs. "general")
- "Happens to"
- "Worth knowing"

---

## Voice anti-modifiers (cut on sight)

- "Stunning" / "incredible" / "amazing" / "gorgeous" — every agent says these
- "Hottest" / "hot market" / "red hot"
- "Exclusive" / "premium" / "elite" — corporate luxury
- "World-class" / "top-tier" / "best-in-class"
- "Synergy" / "leverage" / "ecosystem" — corporate speak
- "Pivot" / "circle back" / "touch base"
- "Strategic partnership" / "Trusted advisor" — clichéd

## What Nikki specifically does NOT say

- "I'd love to..."
- "Let's chat"
- "Touch base"
- "Reach out"
- "Going forward"
- "At the end of the day"
- "100%" (as agreement)
- "Literally" (as emphasis)
- "Just wanted to" (filler)

If draft copy starts with any of these, rewrite the opening.
