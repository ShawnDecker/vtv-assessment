# Pre-List Prep Checklist

> *Luxury without the pretense.*

**Client copy.** Take your time. Some of these matter; some don't. The ones that move your listing's positioning are flagged with a pearl ⚪. The ones that don't are flagged DON'T BOTHER.

---

## The hard truth before any prep

You're about to spend money on prep. Not all of it will come back in your sale price.

After 21 years of valuing Lowcountry homes, here's what comparable sales actually reward — and what they don't.

---

## ⚪ Things worth doing (likely positive ROI)

### Curb appeal (high ROI, often 3-10x return on spend)

- [ ] Fresh mulch, edged beds, weeded garden
- [ ] Power-wash front walkway, porch, siding (especially on Lowcountry humid years)
- [ ] Repaint front door (a deliberate brand-color, not white) — under $150 in paint, often $5-15K in perception
- [ ] Replace dated brass hardware on front door + porch lights
- [ ] Trim or remove anything blocking the front of the home from the curb
- [ ] If applicable: fresh pinestraw under live oaks; trimmed Spanish moss

### Kitchen freshen-up (medium-high ROI)

- [ ] Repaint cabinets if dated color (white shaker performs best in Savannah comparable sales)
- [ ] Replace cabinet hardware (matte black or brass, not aged brass)
- [ ] Replace cabinet door pulls if mismatched
- [ ] Upgrade to a single quality light fixture over the island/sink
- [ ] If counters are over 15 years old and dated material: budget conversation re: replacement

### Primary bathroom (medium ROI)

- [ ] Re-caulk shower / tub
- [ ] Replace dated faucets
- [ ] Replace mirror if dated (frameless modern OR thoughtfully framed in brand-period style)
- [ ] Repaint with neutral
- [ ] Quality light fixture above vanity

### Whole-house quick fixes (low cost, high impression)

- [ ] Repaint any room with strong color or wallpaper to neutral
- [ ] Replace any cracked or chipped switch plates/outlets
- [ ] Touch-up paint scuffs and trim
- [ ] Deep-clean baseboards and door frames
- [ ] Repair any obvious settling cracks (Savannah humidity is real)
- [ ] Replace HVAC filter; verify HVAC age and condition documented for buyer

### Decluttering (FREE, highest ROI of anything on this list)

- [ ] Remove 50% of items from countertops, mantles, bookshelves
- [ ] Remove personal photos from main rooms (selling a home means the buyer needs to picture themselves)
- [ ] Empty closets to 60% capacity (gives illusion of more storage)
- [ ] Pre-pack what you'll be moving anyway

### Photography prep (the day before the photographer arrives)

- [ ] Beds made with neutral linen sets
- [ ] All sinks empty and dry
- [ ] All counters clear except 1-2 deliberate items
- [ ] All toilet seats down
- [ ] All bathroom personal products (toothbrush, etc.) hidden
- [ ] Lights on in every room (yes every)
- [ ] Pets boarded for the morning
- [ ] Cars off the driveway

## DON'T BOTHER (these don't move comparable sales)

- ❌ **Major kitchen remodel** ($30K+) — comparable sales will rarely return $30K of value for $30K of remodel. Refresh, don't replace.
- ❌ **Adding a primary bath suite** unless the existing one is barely functional
- ❌ **New flooring throughout** if existing is in fair condition. Carpet replacement: maybe. Hardwood refinish: only if scratched/dull, NOT a full replacement.
- ❌ **Above-ground pool** — almost never recouped in Savannah
- ❌ **Custom built-ins** — too taste-specific
- ❌ **Highly personalized landscaping** — buyers see maintenance, not beauty
- ❌ **High-end appliance upgrades** unless the kitchen is genuinely high-end and the appliances are part of an actual brand presentation
- ❌ **Aftermarket sound systems** — speakers don't appraise
- ❌ **Above-code insulation upgrades** — energy savings narrative doesn't translate to comparable bracket
- ❌ **Unpermitted finished space** — appraisal won't credit GLA without permits, which affects lender comfort

## Conversations to have BEFORE prep

These are the higher-stakes decisions. Bring them to our pre-list comparable analysis call.

### 1. The "we put $X into it" question

If you've spent significant money on the home, we'll discuss how the comparable sales reward (or don't reward) those specific improvements. The honest answer is more useful than the flattering one.

### 2. Permitted vs unpermitted improvements

Anything not on permit will not be reflected in lender appraisal. We need to know what's permitted before we set the comparable bracket.

### 3. Estate / divorce / inheritance situations

Different rules apply if this sale is driven by estate or divorce. Often a USPAP-compliant appraisal (separate from this listing engagement) is required first. Gergacs Appraisals, LLC handles those.

### 4. Timing

Spring (March-May) is the strongest demand window in Savannah. Fall (September-November) is the second-strongest. Summer is workable for the right property; winter requires patience or a price entry that respects slower velocity. We'll discuss your timing flexibility.
