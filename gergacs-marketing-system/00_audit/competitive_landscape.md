## Three positioning gaps Nikki can own

### A. The Appraiser-Realtor

**NOT ONE** competitor above holds a Certified Residential Appraiser credential. Every agent sells emotion + access; Nikki can sell **comparable-supported pricing analysis grounded in 21 years of appraisal methodology**.

Frame: *"The only Savannah luxury Realtor who values your home before she lists it."*

This neutralizes Sotheby's prestige and Heather Murphy's volume in one move. The "21 years of appraising" data point is unmatchable in Savannah for the foreseeable future.

### B. Lowcountry Lifestyle Curator (not "luxury agent")

Competitors all say "luxury" — none owns a *place-based* identity. "Pearl Awaits" is already pearl/oyster/coast-coded. Lean into Lowcountry-specific lifestyle:
- Tide charts as buyer education
- Oak canopies vs marsh views as lifestyle preferences
- May River vs Wilmington River distinctions
- Bryan vs Effingham trade-offs (taxes, drive time, schools)

Nobody is doing micro-geography content. This is the largest content greenfield in the audit.

### C. The Downsizer & Relocator's Quiet Advisor

Every competitor screams "premier / elite / unmatched." The "without the pretense" line is already a wedge. Position as the **anti-Sotheby's** — calm, credentialed, unhurried.

The downsizer + lifestyle-driven professional persona is largely unclaimed. Heather Murphy's military funnel is the closest — it's volume-led, not concierge-led.

## Five content angles no competitor is publishing

1. **"What an appraiser sees that your Realtor misses"** — walk through 3 Savannah listings showing $40K–$200K valuation gaps an agent would price wrong (historic district carriage house, Tybee 1st-row vs 2nd-row, Ford Plantation lot premium)

2. **"The Pooler vs Richmond Hill vs Effingham downsizer matrix"** — taxes, HOA, drive-time to SAV airport, square-footage-per-dollar, healthcare access. **A spreadsheet, not a vibe post.**

3. **"Why the Sotheby's price isn't always the right price"** — appraiser-led teardown of 3 historic listings explaining over- and under-pricing, without naming brokerages

4. **"Pearl Series: 60 seconds inside a home most agents won't show you"** — Reels showing **flaws first** (settled foundations, flood-zone realities, cypress rot). Radical transparency = differentiation in a market drowning in glamour shots.

5. **"Relocating from [NYC / CHI / DC / ATL] to Savannah — the cost-per-pearl breakdown"** — translate buyer's old cost-of-living into Savannah equivalents. ($2.4M West Village condo = a Tybee oceanfront + an Ardsley Park townhome + 12 years of taxes.)

## Visual differentiation strategy

Lean into **pearl-and-oyster Lowcountry editorial**:
- Warm ivory · oyster-shell gray · deep tidal navy · single accent of brushed brass
- **Reject** Sotheby's black-gold-cream and BHHS cabernet — they're corporate luxury
- **Reject** Cora Bett's italics-and-script — already taken

**Photography style:** soft natural light, asymmetrical composition, lifestyle-in-frame (a hand on a porch railing, a cup of coffee on a marsh dock). NOT the dead-still empty-room real-estate-photographer look every competitor uses.

**Typography:** high-contrast modern serif (think Sangoma or Canela) paired with a humanist sans. Trocchi + Noto Sans (already in her existing brand) qualify.
