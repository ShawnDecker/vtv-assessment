# Savannah Image Library — Real Local Visual Art

Every email, pitch deck, and content piece in this campaign references this library. The rule: **no generic stock real-estate photography.** If a piece of marketing doesn't show real Savannah, it doesn't ship.

---

## The visual DNA

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| Color | Warm ivory · oyster-shell gray · deep tidal navy · brushed brass accent |
| Light | Soft natural — golden hour, blue hour, overcast diffused |
| Composition | Asymmetrical · lifestyle-in-frame · human element when possible |
| Mood | Refined but not stiff · southern but not cliché |
| What to avoid | Empty staged rooms · drone shots without context · generic "luxury" interiors that could be anywhere |

---

## Required scenes (every campaign asset draws from these)

### Downtown / historic
- **Live oaks with Spanish moss** — the iconic Savannah canopy (Bull Street, Ardsley Park, Forsyth Park)
- **Forsyth Park fountain** at sunrise (golden) or blue hour
- **Squares** — Chippewa, Monterey, Lafayette, Pulaski
- **Historic district architecture** — wrought iron, brick, ivy, painted wood
- **Bull Street + Broughton Street** street life
- **Cobblestone + River Street** waterfront
- **St. Patrick's Day Parade** (March)
- **Savannah Music Festival** (March/April)
- **Christmas on Bull Street** lights

### Coastal / Lowcountry
- **Tybee Island beach** — pier, lighthouse, dunes
- **Tybee Island sunrise** — east-facing
- **Marshlands** at golden hour with reflective water
- **Wormsloe Historic Site** — the iconic moss-canopied driveway
- **Skidaway Island** — marina, marsh views
- **The Landings** — golf, water, mature live oaks
- **Wassaw Sound + barrier islands** — boat-perspective
- **Oyster roasts** in fall/winter
- **Shrimp boats** in the rivers

### Suburbs and surrounding
- **Pooler new construction** — modern southern, clean
- **Richmond Hill marsh** — quieter, more residential
- **Habersham (closer to Beaufort SC)** — rural luxury
- **Effingham County** rural farmland edges

### Lifestyle moments (with people)
- **Couples on porches** — multiple ages, multiple races
- **Families** at squares, beach, parks (multi-generational, multi-ethnic)
- **Solo professionals** at coffee shops, walking, working remote
- **Retirees** on bikes, golf carts, walks, dinner together
- **Military families** (Hunter AAF, Fort Stewart) — uniformed and casual
- **First-time buyers** receiving keys
- **Pets** — labs, golden retrievers, retired-dog energy

## Demographic representation requirements

Every campaign asset that includes people MUST include intentional demographic diversity. The rule:

- **Across the campaign as a whole:** parity-or-better representation of Black, White, Hispanic/Latino, Asian, mixed-race; couples and singles; multi-generational families; LGBTQ+ visibility
- **Per email / per deck:** include at least 2 different demographic segments
- **Age range:** 30s through 70s represented across the campaign
- **Family structures:** couples, families with kids, empty-nesters, multi-generational, solo

This is not box-checking. Savannah's actual buyer profile is genuinely diverse — military, corporate relocators, retirees, lifestyle professionals — and the marketing should reflect the city she actually serves.

## Sources (in order of preference)

### Tier 1 — Original photography (preferred)
- **Nikki's own photos** from listings, neighborhoods, client moments (with permission)
- **Commission a Savannah photographer** for an initial shoot (recommend 2-3 hour session, ~$400-700, captures 30-50 keepers across the visual DNA above)
- **Specific Savannah photographers** known to work with brokerages:
  - Search "Savannah real estate photographer" on local IG accounts (search hashtags `#savannahphotographer` `#savannahweddingphotographer` for stylistic match)
  - Local marketing studios: Phoenix Project Photography, Dom Casas, Jade & Matthew Take Pictures
  - SCAD photography graduates often available for commission work

### Tier 2 — Free with attribution
- **Unsplash** — search: `https://unsplash.com/s/photos/savannah-georgia`
  - Verify each photo is actually Savannah (some "Savannah" photos are of other places)
  - Specific to look for: "Forsyth Park," "Tybee Island," "Wormsloe," "Bonaventure Cemetery," "Spanish moss"
- **Pexels** — search: `https://www.pexels.com/search/savannah%20georgia/`
- **Wikimedia Commons** for historical Savannah architecture (older but uncopyrighted)

### Tier 3 — Paid stock (when nothing else fits)
- **Adobe Stock** — best for editorial-style southern lifestyle photography
- **Getty Images Editorial** — for festivals (St. Patrick's Day Parade, Savannah Music Festival) — these are often editorial-only licensed
- **Shutterstock** — broadest selection, weakest editorial feel

### Tier 4 — Last resort
- **Unsplash search URL embed** (random photo per call) — only for placeholder mockups, never for production

## Asset checklist before any campaign piece ships

For every email, post, deck, blog, ad:

- [ ] Image is Savannah / Tybee / Lowcountry — not generic stock
- [ ] Image matches the visual DNA (warm light, asymmetrical, human element where appropriate)
- [ ] If people in image: at least one demographic representation choice is intentional
- [ ] Image has descriptive alt text including specific place name + persona-relevant context
- [ ] Image is licensed (original / Unsplash with attribution / paid stock with license proof) — never scraped from a competitor's site
- [ ] Image resolution is appropriate for delivery (email: 1200px wide, hero on web: 1600px+, social: per platform)

## Fonts and overlay styling for image-text composites

When overlaying text on a Savannah image (for IG carousel, ad creative, deck slides):

- **Headline:** Trocchi 36-48pt or similar high-contrast modern serif (Sangoma / Canela)
- **Subhead:** Noto Sans 16-20pt
- **Color over photo:** ivory `#F6F0E6` or pure white `#FFFFFF`
- **Optional gradient:** linear gradient from `rgba(30, 42, 68, 0.6)` (deep navy) to transparent — improves text legibility on busy photos
- **Pearl mark accent:** small bronze `#7A5B2E` divider line or small "next pearl" sub-tag

Maintain visual consistency: every campaign image should be unmistakably "Gergacs Realty," not just "another luxury real estate post."
