# Audience Personas

Five personas Nikki actually serves. Every campaign asset references at least one persona by name. If a piece of content doesn't speak to a specific persona, it doesn't ship.

---

## 1. The Coastal Relocator — "Eleanor"

**Who:** 45-58, married or solo, professional or recently retired. Moving from NY/NJ/CT/DC/Northern VA/CA. Income $200k-$600k or $1M+ liquid net worth. Works remote or about to.

**Why Savannah:** Tax burden, weather, walkability, access to the coast, Southern hospitality without the small-town feel. Possibly a second home transitioning to primary.

**Stage of search:** 6-18 months before move. Researching neighborhoods, schools (if applicable), short-term-rental rules, hurricane insurance.

**Triggers Nikki content well:**
- Neighborhood deep-dives (Ardsley Park vs Habersham vs Skidaway, with honest tradeoffs)
- Tax / cost-of-living comparisons (NY → GA, especially)
- Short-term-rental ordinance changes (Tybee, downtown)
- Coastal flood / hurricane / insurance basics
- "From [northeast city] to Savannah — what surprised me" client stories

**Channels:** YouTube long-form, blog SEO, Instagram Reels (lifestyle), LinkedIn (for relocating execs)

**Voice register:** Honest, precise, slightly insider. Eleanor has been pitched. She reacts to "I've been doing this 21 years and I'll tell you what most agents won't" more than to "let me find your dream home."

**ManyChat keyword fit:** MOVE, LOCAL

**Drip campaign fit:** Relocation Drip

## 2. The Right-Sizer — "Bill & Sandra"

**Who:** 58-72, kids out of the house, often empty-nest or pre-retirement. Currently in 3000-5000 sqft home. Looking to move to 1500-2200 sqft single-level or low-maintenance.

**Why now:** Kids gone, stairs getting harder, want to spend the next 20 years differently. Sometimes one spouse pushing harder than the other. Often a distance dimension — moving closer to grandchildren, or to the coast.

**Stage of search:** Often "thinking about it for 2 years." Trigger event: a friend downsized, a stair fall, a market peak that makes selling current home attractive.

**Triggers Nikki content well:**
- "What downsizing actually looks like" — specific square footage, cost-of-living, daily life changes
- Senior-friendly neighborhood tours (single-level, low-maintenance, walkable)
- Pre-list valuation content (their current 4000sqft home — what's it really worth)
- Estate planning + appraisal angle (her dual credential)
- Pearl Cottage / Tybee single-level features

**Channels:** Facebook (organic + lookalike ads), email/blog, YouTube (long-form), occasional LinkedIn for retiring professionals

**Voice register:** Patient, reassuring, never pushy. Bill & Sandra have been selling and buying houses for 40 years and don't need education — they need a guide who respects that.

**ManyChat keyword fit:** DOWNSIZE, SELL

**Drip campaign fit:** Downsizing Drip, Pre-List Drip

## 3. The Lifestyle Buyer — "Marcus"

**Who:** 38-52, professional, often single or partnered without kids. $400k-$1.5M target purchase price. Already lives in or near Savannah but renting or in starter home. Wants the *upgrade*.

**Why now:** Career stable, wants the historic cottage / waterfront condo / trophy property they couldn't afford 5 years ago. Sometimes recently divorced and "buying their next chapter."

**Stage of search:** 3-9 months. Will tour weekly when serious. Buys on lifestyle fit more than on spec.

**Triggers Nikki content well:**
- "Three quiet places in [neighborhood] no one tells transplants about"
- Coffee, food, walk, weekend itinerary content (lifestyle Reels)
- Historic district insider knowledge
- Waterfront-specific buying considerations
- The Pearl metaphor lands hardest with Marcus — he picks aesthetics, not specs

**Channels:** Instagram Reels (primary), TikTok, YouTube Shorts, occasional newsletter

**Voice register:** Curated, refined, slightly aspirational. Marcus reacts to taste signals.

**ManyChat keyword fit:** PEARL, LOCAL, PICK, TOUR

**Drip campaign fit:** First-Touch Pearl Drip, Top Picks Weekly

## 4. The Pre-Sell Homeowner — "Carolyn"

**Who:** 50-70, owns a Savannah-area home for 8+ years, considering selling but unsure of value or timing. Often "we'd sell if we got the right number." Sometimes triggered by an inheritance / divorce / health event.

**Why now:** Market signal (heard a neighbor sold for X) + life signal (need / want to move) align.

**Stage of search:** Pre-decision. They are NOT yet a seller — they are someone deciding *whether* to sell.

**Triggers Nikki content well:**
- Pre-list valuation content (her appraiser credential earns trust here in a way no one else can match)
- "What an appraiser sees that an agent misses" educational
- Honest market data: "the median price in Ardsley Park last 90 days was X — here's what's moving and what's sitting"
- Cost-to-prepare-for-sale walkthroughs (paint, staging, repairs — what's worth it, what isn't)
- Estate / divorce / retirement-trigger appraisal content (sensitively)

**Channels:** Blog SEO (specific neighborhood + "what's my home worth"), email to past clients, Facebook organic (community group presence), targeted Meta ads to homeowners 5+ years tenured

**Voice register:** Trusted advisor. Carolyn doesn't want a sales pitch — she wants honest data and a calm presence. The pearl metaphor lands medium here; her dual credential is the headline.

**ManyChat keyword fit:** SELL

**Drip campaign fit:** Pre-List Drip

**Special:** Lane 1 Sales ad #1 (Pre-List Valuation) targets Carolyn primarily.

## 5. The Past Client — "Maggie & Tom"

**Who:** Anyone Nikki has helped buy or sell in the last 8 years. Mix of buyer-only, seller-only, and both-sides clients.

**Why they matter now:** Average homeowner moves every 7-13 years. A client from 2018-2020 is approaching their next move window. Plus referrals: every happy past client knows 1-3 people thinking about Savannah real estate.

**Triggers Nikki content well:**
- *Personal* outreach (not mass email): a real check-in, a relevant article, a remembered detail about their family
- Holiday / anniversary touches (move-in date, birthday, etc.)
- Market value updates ("just out of curiosity, here's what your home is probably worth now")
- Genuine community content (events, local features) — the kind they'd want to forward

**Channels:** Outlook personal (NEVER mass email these), occasional handwritten note, in-person at events, Trello reminders for cadence

**Voice register:** Warm, specific, no-ask-implied. Every touch should feel like she remembered them, not like she's working a list.

**ManyChat keyword fit:** None — past clients shouldn't be hitting IG keywords; they should be on personal cadence

**Drip campaign fit:** Manual cadence in Trello, NOT BoldTrail drip (drip campaigns to past clients feel cheap)

**Special:** Lane 3 Relational lane is built for Maggie & Tom.

## How to use these personas operationally

When briefing any new asset (post, ad, blog, email):

1. State which persona it's for (one persona per asset; if it tries to address multiple, it'll address none)
2. Reference 1-2 specific persona triggers from above
3. Match the voice register
4. Pick the channel that fits the persona × content type
5. Pick the ManyChat keyword + drip if it's lead-capture content

If a piece of content can't pass step 1 (no clear persona), it doesn't ship.
