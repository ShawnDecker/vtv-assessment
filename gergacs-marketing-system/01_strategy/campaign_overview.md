# Campaign Overview — 30 Days, 3 Lanes, One Brand

## The thesis

Most real estate marketing fails because it picks one lane and starves the others. A pure direct-response engine burns through trust. A pure brand-content engine never generates a single Friday consult. A pure relationship engine plateaus at the size of the existing SOI.

This campaign runs **three lanes simultaneously**, each with its own goal, KPI, and tempo. Together they protect each other: when ad performance dips, the content lane keeps the audience warm. When content stalls, the relational lane pulls referrals. When the SOI cools, the sales lane re-fills.

## Why each lane is built this way

### Lane 1 — Sales

**Premise:** With 18 BoldTrail drips already built and ManyChat keywords mapped, the bottleneck isn't nurture — it's *getting more people into nurture*. Lane 1 spends money to fill the top of funnel.

**Tactical core:** Two Meta ad sets running concurrently:
- **Pre-List Comparable Analysis ad** — targeting Savannah/Tybee/Pooler homeowners who've been in their home 5+ years. Hook leads with the appraiser credential.
- **Relocation Discovery ad** — targeting people relocating from NY/NJ/DC/Northern VA/CA to GA. Hook leads with the local-native angle.

Both ads route to ManyChat (not a landing page) — keyword PEARL or MOVE — because her keyword infrastructure is already live and cheaper to test than a new funnel.

**Sequence:**
- Day 1-3: Ad creative briefed and built (Coffee & Contracts assets + Canva)
- Day 4: Ads launch in Meta Business Suite
- Day 5-30: ManyChat captures, BoldTrail drips, Microsoft Bookings closes
- Day 14 + Day 28: Performance review, kill or scale per ad

### Lane 2 — Marketing

**Premise:** Audience is the moat. Every follower today is a lead next year. Lane 2 builds the audience that compounds while Lane 1 spends on the audience that doesn't yet exist.

**Tactical core:** A single content engine producing 4-5 pieces per week distributed across IG, YT, blog, TikTok via Repurpose.io:

- **Reels (3/wk):** neighborhood spotlights, pearl-metaphor educational, behind-the-scenes
- **YouTube long-form (1/wk):** 5-10 minute home tours or "ask the appraiser" episodes
- **Blog (1/wk):** SEO-targeted neighborhood deep-dive or guide post
- **TikTok (3/wk):** Reel cross-post via Repurpose.io
- **Facebook (2/wk):** community + listing posts, pulled from IG via Metricool

**Cadence:**
- Mondays: Brief pulled from Metricool + BoldTrail (the Monday Brief from her ops package)
- Tuesday: Drafts in Notion Lighthouse
- Wednesday: Build day in Coffee & Contracts + Canva
- Thursday: Schedule for the week in Metricool
- Friday-Sunday: Posts go live, comments/DMs handled with ManyChat

### Lane 3 — Relational

**Premise:** A boutique brokerage's biggest unfair advantage is past clients who genuinely like the agent. The cost of acquiring a referred client is roughly 1/8 of a paid lead. Lane 3 systematically activates this.

**Tactical core:**
- **Past-client touch** — every Wednesday, Nikki sends 5 personal Outlook emails to past clients (rotating through the SOI list pulled from BoldTrail). No ask, just a check-in or genuine relevant share.
- **Review request sweep** — for every closed deal in the past 90 days, send the structured review request via Outlook (Google + Zillow + Facebook one-click links).
- **One client event** — month-end coastal coffee or oyster roast with 8-10 past clients. Low-key, high-trust.
- **Referral activation** — for every past client who's mentioned a friend "thinking of moving," call them personally within 48 hours. Make the introduction warm.

## The interlock

The lanes feed each other:

- **Lane 1 → Lane 2:** Every booked consult becomes content. "Just spent the morning at a Skidaway estate getting it ready to list — here's what I look for first." (anonymous)
- **Lane 2 → Lane 1:** Every Reel and YouTube viewer becomes a retargetable audience for the Lane 1 ads.
- **Lane 3 → Lane 1:** Every referral lands directly in BoldTrail with `referral` tag — same drip + Microsoft Bookings flow as a Lane 1 lead but with 3x close rate.
- **Lane 3 → Lane 2:** Past clients featured (with permission) in YouTube tour videos and blog posts become the most credible content on the channel.

## The 30-day rhythm

```
Week 1 — Foundation
    Mon-Wed: Audit reviews + lane briefings
    Thu: Sales lane ads built (Coffee & Contracts + Canva)
    Fri: Sales lane goes live · Relational lane "5 emails" rhythm starts
    Weekend: Marketing lane content backlog built (4-5 pieces)

Week 2 — Scale
    Sales lane: first performance pass · double down or kill creative
    Marketing lane: first full week of cadence (3 Reels, 1 YT, 1 blog)
    Relational lane: review sweep complete

Week 3 — Optimize
    Sales lane: second creative variant launched
    Marketing lane: best-performing piece from week 2 amplified (paid boost)
    Relational lane: client event invitations sent

Week 4 — Convert + Reflect
    Sales lane: retargeting layer added (warm audience from weeks 1-3)
    Marketing lane: review what's pulling, plan month 2
    Relational lane: client event held · referrals chased
    Last day: Full 30-day report + month-2 brief
```

## What success looks like at day 30

- 8-15 booked consults in the calendar (mix of buyer + seller + appraisal)
- 3-5 active engagements (not yet closed but moving)
- 1 closed deal directly attributed to the campaign (lower bound)
- IG followers up 25-40%
- 100+ newsletter subscribers added
- 3+ blog posts published, 1-2 already ranking
- 10+ Google/Zillow/Facebook reviews collected
- 2-4 referrals from past clients
- 1 client event held

If 6 of 9 of these hit, the campaign was a win. If 8 of 9 hit, raise budget for month 2 and replicate.

## What you don't optimize for

- Vanity follower count without engagement
- "Likes" on individual posts
- Gross impressions
- Newsletter open rate above 40% (above that, it's bots, not engagement)
- Pretty dashboards

The scoreboard is consults booked, deals closed, referrals received. Everything else is leading indicator.
