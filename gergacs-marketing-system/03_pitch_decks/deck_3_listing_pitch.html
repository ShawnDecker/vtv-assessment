What I look for
06 / 12

The first 60 seconds in your home.

Before any comparable is pulled, I notice:

  • The roof line — original heart-pine vs replacement; period-correct vs aftermarket
  • The foundation lines — Savannah humidity, settling patterns, drainage
  • The crown molding and trim — original is rare and supports the bracket
  • The kitchen "decade signal" — when was it last meaningfully updated
  • The primary bath — most undervalued or overvalued room in pricing
  • The natural light — angle, exposure, time-of-day affecting your photos
  • The yard's intended use — entertaining vs maintenance vs garden

None of these are deal-breakers. All of them inform where your home positions in the comparable bracket.

The hard truths
07 / 12

Things sellers swear add value — that comparables don't actually support.

  • Custom paint colors — unless it's neutralized for sale, it's a discount
  • Above-ground pools — almost never recouped in coastal Georgia
  • Aftermarket sound systems — speakers don't appraise
  • Over-improved primary baths — past a point, the comparables stop rewarding it
  • Highly personalized landscaping — buyers see maintenance, not beauty
  • Unpermitted finished spaces — appraisal won't credit GLA without permits
  • Sentimental "we put $80k into the kitchen" — what matters is what comparables show, not what was spent

I'd rather tell you the honest answer than the flattering one.
