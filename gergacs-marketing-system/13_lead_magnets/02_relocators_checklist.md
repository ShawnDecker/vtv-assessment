# The Savannah Relocator's Checklist

> *From a Savannah-native Realtor and GA Certified Residential Appraiser of 21 years.*
> *Luxury without the pretense.*

Two checklists in one document.

The first is what to verify *before* you sign anything. Skip these and you'll discover them painfully in your first 90 days.

The second is what to do in your first 90 days. The relocators who land well work this list. The ones who land hard skip it.

---

## PART ONE · 12 things to verify before you sign anything

### 1. Hurricane and wind insurance — get an actual quote

Not an estimate. A quote. From a licensed Georgia broker. For the specific address.

Why: coastal Georgia properties carry meaningful premiums you've probably never carried before. The number can swing your monthly carrying cost by hundreds.

Action: get the quote *before* your offer goes in.

### 2. FEMA flood zone — pull the map for the specific address

URL: [msc.fema.gov](https://msc.fema.gov/portal/home)

Why: Tybee homes are mostly in flood zones. Some Skidaway are. Some inland homes you wouldn't expect are. Almost all coastal properties touch one.

Action: pull the FEMA map. If in zone AE, VE, or X-shaded, get a separate flood insurance quote.

### 3. Property tax math (and which county)

Verify mill rates with the relevant county tax assessor. Chatham (Savannah, Pooler, Tybee) is highest. Bryan (Richmond Hill) is lower. Effingham is lowest.

Why: on a $650K home, the annual delta between counties can be $2,000-$4,000.

### 4. School zoning (even if you don't have kids)

Affects resale. School zoning is granular — even within neighborhoods, individual blocks zone to different schools.

Action: verify with the relevant county school district for the exact address. Don't trust marketing materials.

### 5. HOA fees and any special assessments

Some communities have annual assessments AND sub-association dues AND optional club memberships. The Landings on Skidaway has all three.

Action: get the full breakdown in writing. Verify whether any current special assessments are pending.

### 6. STR (short-term rental) qualification — if relevant

Especially for Tybee. The City of Tybee STR ordinance has changed multiple times in recent years.

Action: don't assume rental income in your investment math without confirming the specific property's current eligibility under current rules.

### 7. The drive-time reality

Test it during rush hour, not at noon. Pooler at 5 PM is different from Pooler at 11 AM. Tybee in summer is different from Tybee in February.

Action: actually drive your route on a Tuesday at 5:30 PM before you commit.

### 8. Hurricane evacuation plan

Where you'll go. How long the drive is. Whether your insurance covers temporary lodging.

Why: not all years have hurricane events, but the years that do, the evacuation is real and immediate.

Action: pick your inland destination (typically Macon-area) before hurricane season begins.

### 9. The actual condition of HVAC and roof

Especially in older homes. Savannah humidity is a real annual stressor.

Action: get a thorough inspection (ideally with thermal imaging for older homes). Verify HVAC age, ducting condition, and roof age explicitly.

### 10. Permitted vs unpermitted improvements

Especially in Ardsley Park, Tybee, and historic-district homes. Unpermitted finished space won't be credited in lender appraisal.

Action: ask for permits on every major improvement. If unavailable, factor in the value implication.

### 11. The neighborhood's seasonal rhythm

Walk the neighborhood at three different times of day. Visit during pollen season (March-April) AND hurricane season (August-October) AND winter.

Why: light, traffic, smell, neighbors-out-on-porches, and overall feel all shift dramatically by season.

### 12. The water situation specifically

Not just "is there a leak." The drainage during heavy rain. The crawl-space moisture. The water-table interaction with the foundation. Lowcountry humidity is real.

Action: visit the property after a heavy rain if possible. Verify drainage. Inspect crawl space.

## PART TWO · 30 things to do in your first 90 days

### Days 1-7

- [ ] Forward mail at usps.com/move
- [ ] Set up utilities under your name (Georgia Power 1-888-660-5890, water/sewer per municipality, internet)
- [ ] Find and label main water shutoff, breaker panel, gas shutoff (if applicable)
- [ ] Re-key the locks (locksmith recommendations available — ask Nikki)
- [ ] Update DMV (you have 30 days to register vehicles in GA)
- [ ] Update voter registration
- [ ] Update bank, insurance, employer, IRS address
- [ ] Note your trash/recycling pickup days

### Weeks 2-4

- [ ] Find your providers: pediatrician, dentist, vet, optometrist (don't wait until you need one)
- [ ] Walk your property edges; note anything not visible in the inspection
- [ ] Test all major appliances; note warranty status
- [ ] Set up local accounts: Publix BOGO, Kroger Plus, Lowes Pro, Home Depot
- [ ] Find your hardware store (Crisman's True Value on Bull, Maxwell's on Habersham, etc.)
- [ ] Find your coffee place. Stick with it.
- [ ] Find your Sunday breakfast place

### Month 2

- [ ] Meet your direct neighbors. Door-knock on day 30 with cookies if appropriate.
- [ ] Find Nextdoor for your neighborhood. Lurk for two weeks before posting.
- [ ] Find your Saturday farmers market (Forsyth Square most prominent)
- [ ] Schedule your first HVAC service (maintain the warranty + Savannah humidity demands attention)
- [ ] Schedule pest control (palmetto bugs are normal; quarterly service is reasonable)
- [ ] Establish primary care MD; transfer prescriptions
- [ ] Visit Tybee at sunrise once
- [ ] Visit Wormsloe Historic Site (you'll see why)
- [ ] Take a tour boat from River Street

### Month 3

- [ ] Take inventory of what you actually use vs what's in storage from your move
- [ ] Schedule a deep-clean before guests arrive (or you'll regret it)
- [ ] If you have a pool: confirm chemical balance + filter cycle settings for Lowcountry humidity
- [ ] If hurricane season: verify shutters install, generator function, evacuation plan
