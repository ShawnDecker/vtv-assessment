Her Story
About
From Pain to Purpose

Dr. Charnika P. Elliott is a devoted wife, nurturing mother, esteemed educator, visionary entrepreneur, international best-selling author, and empowering life coach. Born and raised in Roanoke, Virginia, she has dedicated over 23 years to working hands-on with youth — transforming her deepest personal pain into the community's greatest gain.

After enduring the devastating loss of burying two baby boys a year apart, Charnika became more engulfed in her mission to encourage, motivate, and empower. In March 2005, she walked away from public school teaching to found the Noah-Christian Community Center — named in memory of her sons. In 2011, that vision grew into Noah-Christian Academy, a Christian school where 85% of students test one to two grade levels above the norm.

She is the founder of the H.E.A.L. (Heartache Endured After Loss) Support Group, a life coach to families who've suffered fetal and infant loss, and co-founder of BCElliott & Associates Inc. with her husband Byron. Her book Silence of the Womb: From Pain to a Promise has touched readers across the world, and her speaking ministry inspires audiences to turn pain into purpose.
