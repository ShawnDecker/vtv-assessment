Her Story
About
From the Apollo Theater to the National Stage

At just 13 years old, Joy Sutton took the stage at the World Famous Apollo Theater in New York City. That fearless, purpose-driven spirit has defined every chapter of her career since. Inspired by Oprah Winfrey, she pursued journalism with a singular focus: telling stories that transform lives.

After earning her BA in Journalism from Howard University and MA in Health Promotions from Virginia Tech, Joy spent over a decade as the health reporter and anchor for CBS affiliate WDBJ — covering the Clinton impeachment hearings, the Virginia Tech shootings, and producing special reports from the CDC, Shepherd Center, and Walter Reed Army Medical Center.

She transitioned to corporate communications at HCA Southwest Virginia, then founded Sutton Impact, LLC in 2011 — producing a TV talk show that aired four seasons on a network affiliate. Her client roster includes the Black and Missing Foundation, Calling All Allies Project, and Career Thrivers.

Today, as VP of Brand & Communications for American Addiction Centers, Joy hosts and executive produces the Telly Award-winning "Addiction Talk" — featured in People Magazine, E! News, Yahoo News, and Daily Mail. She also produces the CrimeCon CLUE Award-winning "Untold Stories: Black and Missing" podcast and hosts "She's Big Time Now" — interviewing successful women and sharing their secrets to the top.
