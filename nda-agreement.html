RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company has developed proprietary technology, methodologies, business strategies, and intellectual property related to its P.I.N.K. Framework (People, Influence, Numbers, Knowledge, with Time as a 0.1x-2.0x multiplier), assessment platform, coaching systems, and related products and services;

WHEREAS, the Recipient desires to receive certain Confidential Information for the purpose of evaluating a potential business relationship, investment opportunity, partnership, or collaboration with the Company;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1. "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by the Company to the Recipient, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

  • The P.I.N.K. Assessment Framework, scoring algorithms, Time Multiplier formulas, and cross-pillar impact matrices
  • Patent-pending innovations, provisional patent applications, and IP filing strategies
  • Software source code, system architecture, API designs, database schemas, and technical infrastructure
  • BiaBox multi-agent system architecture and AI/ML models
  • Book-to-System conversion pipeline and licensing methodology
  • Financial projections, revenue models, pricing strategies, and unit economics
  • Customer data, user metrics, subscriber information, and engagement analytics
  • Business plans, investor materials, pitch decks, and prospectus documents
  • Marketing strategies, go-to-market plans, and partnership agreements
  • Trade secrets, proprietary processes, and competitive intelligence
  • Any materials marked "Confidential," "Proprietary," or similar designation

1.2. Confidential Information does not include information that:

  • (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Recipient;
  • (b) Was known to the Recipient prior to disclosure, as documented by written records;
  • (c) Is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Confidential Information;
  • (d) Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure.
2. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECIPIENT

2.1. The Recipient shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose, publish, or otherwise disseminate any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company.

2.2. The Recipient shall use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating a potential business relationship with the Company (the "Permitted Purpose") and for no other purpose whatsoever.

2.3. The Recipient shall protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

2.4. The Recipient shall limit access to Confidential Information to those of its employees, agents, or advisors who (a) need to know such information for the Permitted Purpose, and (b) are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those contained herein.

2.5. The Recipient shall not reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble any technology, software, or systems disclosed as part of the Confidential Information.

2.6. The Recipient shall not copy, reproduce, or duplicate any Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Permitted Purpose.

3. NON-COMPETE & NON-SOLICITATION

3.1. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following its termination, the Recipient shall not directly or indirectly develop, market, or sell any product or service that is substantially similar to or competitive with the Company's P.I.N.K. Assessment Framework, cross-pillar coaching methodology, or Book-to-System conversion pipeline.

3.2. During the term and for twelve (12) months thereafter, the Recipient shall not solicit, recruit, or hire any employee, contractor, or advisor of the Company, or induce any such person to terminate their relationship with the Company.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1. No disclosure of Confidential Information shall be construed as granting any license, right, title, or interest in or to any intellectual property of the Company.

4.2. All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

4.3. Any feedback, suggestions, or improvements proposed by the Recipient regarding the Confidential Information shall be the exclusive property of the Company, and the Recipient hereby assigns all rights therein to the Company.

5. TERM & DURATION

5.1. This Agreement shall be effective as of the date first written above and shall continue for a period of three (3) years from the date of disclosure of the last Confidential Information.

5.2. The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for the full three-year period.

5.3. Trade secrets shall be protected for as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law, which may extend beyond the three-year period.

6. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF MATERIALS

6.1. Upon written request by the Company, or upon termination of discussions between the parties, the Recipient shall promptly (within ten (10) business days):

  • (a) Return all documents, materials, and tangible items containing Confidential Information; or
  • (b) Destroy all such materials and certify in writing that destruction is complete.

6.2. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Recipient may retain one (1) archival copy solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to continued confidentiality obligations.

7. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES

7.1. The Recipient may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Recipient:

  • (a) Gives the Company prompt written notice of the required disclosure (to the extent legally permitted);
  • (b) Cooperates with the Company in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy;
  • (c) Discloses only the minimum amount of Confidential Information required.

7.2. The Recipient may disclose Confidential Information to its legal and financial advisors, provided such advisors are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than this Agreement.

8. REMEDIES

8.1. The Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy.

8.2. In the event of any breach or threatened breach, the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other equitable remedies, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

8.3. The prevailing party in any legal action arising from this Agreement shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

9. GENERAL PROVISIONS

9.1. Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Virginia, without regard to conflicts of law principles.

9.2. Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.

9.3. Amendments. No modification of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both parties.

9.4. Severability. If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

9.5. Waiver. No failure or delay in exercising any right under this Agreement shall constitute a waiver of that right.

9.6. Assignment. The Recipient may not assign this Agreement without the Company's prior written consent. The Company may assign this Agreement to any successor entity.

9.7. Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronic signatures, each of which shall constitute an original.

9.8. No Obligation. Nothing in this Agreement shall obligate either party to enter into any further agreement, transaction, or business relationship.
